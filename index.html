Portfolio Reflection

This portfolio building assignment has taught me a lot in terms of website design, and gave me the opportunity to use the skills I am currently learning in my Web Based IT class. I am incredibly happy with how was I able to take a simple Bootstrap template and turn it into something of my own using the basic layout, customizing and expanding it as I see fit with my own stylistic flair. The Ruby Star Red theming isn't too obnoxious and gives enough contrast with the otherwise white and grey theming. The website is easy to use, being able to scroll down or to click on the quick navigations on the side panel for each different section. The font selection is very simple with the focus of being legible. The font size changes and bolds to distinguish different headings and the paragraphs to make it easier to distinguish and aiding in the task to make it as easy on the eye as possible.

Overall, I have learned that it isn't as difficult as I thought to create a website of my own. Using the knowledge from my other classes on how to use Bootstrap and mess with HTML, CSS, JavaScript and best practices of design gave me a good base. This assignment helped me to use my knowledge to create a functioning product that I am proud of making, as well as understanding how something like a digital portfolio could be useful for interviews and showing off to the recruiters. The most helpful resources I had used in this project were the slides from my Web Development class and HCI class, as well as W3C resources that helped me with more specific concerns.
